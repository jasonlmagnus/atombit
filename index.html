Click to flip
Why it's less helpful
Mission needs a cause people rally behind
"Fix the ROI problem" is commercially compelling but not emotionally galvanising. Missionary brands need a broader purpose that clients and employees genuinely believe in. This feels more like a proof point than a mission.

Our recommendation: Lead with Disruptor — it reframes the buying criteria entirely. Layer in Real & Human as the essential flavour that makes atombit feel different from faceless consultancies. The others have merit but are either too familiar (Next Gen, Missionary) or too limiting (Misfit, Maverick).

Our Approach

How we run workshops

Environment matters.

We create the conditions where real thinking happens. Where people leave the room having genuinely shifted—not just ticked a box.
Place Top of The Shard. Off-site retreats. Spaces that shift perspective.
Customer Personas in the room. Rapid validation built into every session.
Practical Show me, don't tell me. Paper prototypes before the day ends.
Active Games and time-boxed tasks. Not debates and decks.

Between the four of us, we've facilitated hundreds of creative workshops across every kind of organization you can imagine. From senior leadership teams to company-wide engagements, from startups finding their footing to global brands reimagining their future.

What we've learned is that no two challenges call for the same approach. So we've built a deep toolbox of techniques and we select the right ones for your needs and your culture.

Sometimes that means props and provocation. Sometimes it means bringing in actors to make an idea visceral. Sometimes it means storytelling that reframes how people see a problem. We've run sessions that gaze boldly into the future—and others that ask harder questions about what's right in front of us.

Interactivity is everything. Real breakthroughs don't happen in PowerPoint. They happen when people are on their feet, competing, creating, surprising themselves.
Location matters. We take people out of their everyday environment. Different spaces unlock different thinking. From The Shard to countryside retreats.
Show me, don't tell me. Ideas become real before lunch. Paper prototypes, smartphone demos, tangible outputs — not slide decks and strategy documents.
